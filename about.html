ALL ABOUT RYAN

Self Intro

My name is Ryan Lee and I graduated from ACS International school in Singapore in 2020 with an IB Diploma. I love to play sports especially soccer, basketball and baseball. I was a baseball player during my primary school age, and I was one of the members of the ACS school soccer and basketball team. I also like to watch sports matches. I am really passionate about analyzing all the statistics and the records of players so I used to memorize all the statistics in baseball.
Beyond sports, I was also an active member in the department of the Students Affairs in the 15th Prefectorial board. As a board member of the Student Affairs department, I played a key role in student welfare and organised a school-wide treasure hunt for bonding between students and teachers. I am a volunteer at a social club, where we care for local elderly people and spend time with them to relieve loneliness. I was also selected as the Korea Representative for a creativity competition called ‘Odyssey of the Mind’ Olympiad.
Combined, these experiences have developed my teamwork, leadership, critical thinking and creativity, all of which are vital to becoming a well-rounded student. I also have a lot of fruitful and amazing experiences of medical volunteering with my father and his hospital staff.

Q&A

Who’s your role model?

My role model is my father. My father is a neurosurgeon who has a strong responsibility towards his family and patients. He also brings happiness for his patients. I have used to follow medical services with my father from an early age. I have seen how my father treated patients who are suffering from poverty. He has sympathized to the patients and touched each of them with all his heart. I was impressed and he was the person who inspired me to be a medical missionary doctor. Thanks to my father, I am able to maintain my vision until now.

What’s the most inspiring line/ speech for you?

There is a quote, “No pain, no gain”. It seems a little bit simple but this quote contains huge and depth meaning. I have really understood this quote when I was studying. In order to be one of the best academic performed students, I got to put a lot of effort on it to achieve my goal. However, in that process, it requires bloodthirsty efforts. After I put all my effort into it, I finally achieved my goal and I really felt proud of myself. Let me be more specific. The time when I just started IB diploma, I was so eager to be an orientation organization leader. I prepared the interview for three days before the real one. I put a huge effort into it and finally I got permission to get into an orientation organization leader. It was one of my memorable moment and I could not forget that time.

What challenges have you undertaken?

*Diligence, commitment, not giving up It was my first abroad since I got the acceptance letter from my school, ACS International school. As it was my first abroad in my life, I was slightly worried if I could adapt well on new environment out of my country. There was some cultural and language barrier to overcome but for me, it was quite easy to overcome those things because I used to go to international schools in Korea and I really like to be friends with people especially international students. I kept on smiling and be nice to the students and that made me overcome those challenges.

Me & My family

Family is the most important and invaluable gift in my life. I have been able to accomplish this much is mainly because of my family’s unwavering support. My father, a neurosurgeon, inspired me to pursue an education in medicine and a career in healthcare, by bringing me on volunteer trips to Indonesia, Cambodia and Mongolia. These trips made me realise that many people did not have access to healthcare. This inspired me to want to provide healthcare. He has also given me great insight into the medical field, especially neuroscience. His sincerity and dedication towards his job are the main reasons why he is my role model. My mother also showers me with love. She is always beside me when I am sick or hurt. She has also helped me in gathering information about universities I can attend or pathways I can take to achieve my dreams. She has always supported me, which I am always grateful for. I also have a younger sister and a younger brother. When I am tired and worn out, they are like chocolates to me as I am energised when I spend time with them. They always make me smile, and in return I want to always be able to take care of them. As an eldest son, I have a strong responsibility to take care of my siblings as well as my parents. My family is my main pillar of support, and is always there for me through my ups and downs. They motivate me to work hard and achieve success in life, so that I can repay their love and support.
