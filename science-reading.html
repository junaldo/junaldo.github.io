“Life At Extremes Chapter 6 - The Final Frontier”

2018/12/12 | By Junhyuk Lee

This chapter is talking about a way to survive in a vacuum of space. A lot of people in long time ago had attempted to go to a space for exploration.
Finally, on 20 July 1969, Neil Armstrong went to the moon for the first time in the world. However, since then, there is no man who went again to the moon because there are so many risks to go to space. Going out of space gives human a serious physiological problems. Firstly, a space flight requires the acceleration to escape the Earth’s gravity which imposes an additional gravitational force on the body. Second problem is weightlessness. This can cause disabling motion sickness, the redistribution of fluids around the body, and it decreases the amount of red blood cells. It also weakens bones and loss muscle mass.

Life has to be protected from huge temperature variations, airlessness, and radiation. As well as practical problems it poses for everyday living, weightlessness affects the balance and induces muscle and bone loss. And then of course there are the accelerations ("g-forces") which is found in bungee jumping and high-performance military aircraft are necessary to climb into orbit and the problems astronauts face on re-entry and return to a normal environment.

“Chapter 7 - The Outer Space”

2018/12/12 | By Junhyuk Lee

This chapter is talking about the world of microbes. Unlike the other chapters which talked about the human life, this chapter explains about the non-human life, especially in bacteria aspects. There is a phrase called the tree of life. The tree of life contains three main branches: the eukaryotes, bacteria, and archaea. The eukaryotes are the cells that possess nucleus which is the storehouse of DNA. Humans, animals and all kinds of unicellular are considered as the eukaryotes. The bacteria which is also called prokaryotes are the cells that do not possess nucleus inside and examples for it is cyanobacteria. Both bacteria and archaea are single-celled organisms but archaea is a specialized branch of the bacteria. Both bacteria and archaea thrive even in boiling water, caustic soda lakes, in strong acid, in highly salty water, at enormous pressure and in deep middle of rocks. They also playing a role of cleaning the environment and produce energy. Cells typically die at temperature over 50 degree and celsius because proteins inside cells will all be denatured.

“Biology extended essay”

2018/12/12 | By Junhyuk Lee

Topic: How are the antibacterial effects of Allicin influenced by changes in temperature(25°C, 30°C, 40°C, 50°C, 60°C) and exposure to open air(10 minutes, 40 minutes), as measured by the percentage absorption of light with a spectrometer?

For my Extended Essay for my International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme, I wrote a 4000-word essay on the antibacterial effects of Allicin, the active compound in garlic. I researched the topic using textbooks from libraries and journal articles available online. This independent research gave me insight into academic life. I found the topic complex and some papers difficult to understand but I was amazed at how much information I could absorb, giving me a taste of becoming an expert in a field. I was especially intrigued as to how Allicin is used as a treatment in rural areas of the world without access to western medicine. I witnessed traditional medicine use first-hand as a volunteer medical support officer in South East Asia. This opened my eyes to how traditional techniques can inspire modern medicine, such as in the discovery of artemisinin for malaria treatment.
